The Role

Judge Responsibilities

Every judge on the Buddies panel commits to a set of core responsibilities that keep the competition fair, celebrated, and true to the community's values.

Story & Author Evaluation
Thoroughly read and assess all assigned SOTY entries and AOTY qualifiers using the official Buddies scoring rubric. Every score must be accompanied by thoughtful reasoning and constructive notes where required.
Confidentiality & Discretion
All deliberations, scores, and internal discussions are strictly confidential until results are officially announced. Judges must not hint at, leak, or discuss outcomes with anyone outside the panel; including close friends.
Bias Declaration
If a judge has a personal connection to a competitor; friendship, collaboration, or any relationship that might compromise neutrality, they must declare this immediately and recuse themselves from that specific evaluation.
Active Participation
Judges are expected to be present and responsive throughout the competition season (October–December). Missed deadlines or unexplained absences may result in a judge being replaced to protect the integrity of the competition.
Community Representation
As public-facing members of the competition, judges represent Buddies at all times. Respectful, encouraging, and professional conduct online - especially during the competition period is required.
Collaborative Deliberation
Judges participate in panel discussions to align on borderline cases and ensure scoring consistency. Healthy debate is encouraged; the goal is always to reach the most accurate and fair outcome for every competitor.
Selection Criteria

Requirements to Be a Judge

Not anyone can sit on the panel. These are the qualities and commitments we look for when selecting judges for SOTY & AOTY.

01 📖
Deep Community Knowledge
A strong, active understanding of Episode stories, authors, and the culture of the Episode community. Judges must know what excellence looks like in this specific creative space.
02 ⚖️
Neutrality & Impartiality
The ability to set aside personal preferences, friendships, and opinions to evaluate every entry on merit alone. Bias of any kind, including unconscious bias - disqualifies a judge from specific evaluations.
03 ✍️
Creative Experience
Experience in writing, reviewing, directing, or critically analyzing Episode stories is strongly preferred. Judges who understand what makes a story worth reading are also able to recognize the challenges behind crafting it and what ultimately makes it exceptional.
04 🌟
Good Standing & Reputation
A clean, respected presence within the Episode and Buddies community. Judges must be known for respectful conduct, positive engagement, and uplifting others; not conflict or controversy.
05 🗓️
Full Season Availability
Availability to actively participate from October through December, including panel check-ins, scoring deadlines, and deliberation sessions. This role requires consistent commitment due to its level of responsibility.
06 💬
Communication & Teamwork
Judges must respond promptly, communicate clearly with the panel, and contribute constructively to group discussions. The best outcomes come from a team that listens as much as it speaks.
07 🔒
Commitment to Confidentiality
Strict discretion is required throughout the competition. Judges must be able to hold information; scores, deliberations, and outcomes - without sharing any details until official announcements are made.
08 ❤️
Passion for Storytelling
Beyond the mechanics, judges must genuinely love stories and care about the authors who create them. That passion shows in every score, every note, and every decision; and competitors deserve nothing less.
09 🧠
Critical Thinking
The ability to think analytically and evaluate a story or author on multiple dimensions simultaneously; plot, directing, community presence, writing craft - while remaining coherent, consistent, and fair.
